Freddie Mercury

Freddie Mercury was a British singer, songwriter and producer,
best known as the lead vocalist and songwriter of the rock band Queen. 
He was born in 5 September 1946, in the British protectorate of Sultanate of Zanzibar, East Africa.
In 2002, Mercury was placed at number 58 in the BBC's poll of the 100 Greatest Britons.
