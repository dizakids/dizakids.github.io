	above sentence types. Extract from Regeneration by Pat Barker Burns. Rivers had become adept at finding 
	bearable aspects to unbearable experiences, but Burns defeated him. What had happened to him was so vile, 
	so disgusting, that Rivers could find no redeeming feature.
	He'd been thrown into the air by the explosion of a shell and had landed, head-first, on a German corpse,
	whose gas-filled belly had ruptured on impact. Before Burns lost consciousness, he'd had time torealize

	as Rivers had often seen him, retching up the last ounce of bile, hardly looked like a human being at all.

	His suffering was without purpose or dignity, and yes. Rivers knew exactly what Burns meant when he said 
	it was a joke.
